Why the contours curve: R(m,n) = R(n,m) forces every iso-value line to be symmetric across the diagonal. Growth accelerates in both directions simultaneously, so equal-value curves bend outward — like wavefronts expanding from (1,1).

This is exactly level-set thinking: each contour is a surface of constant "energy" R(m,n) = k. The gradient of R points radially outward, just like ∇V(h) in Livnium points toward the nearest anchor.
